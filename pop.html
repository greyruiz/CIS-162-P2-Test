My Growth


I went a full 360 when it comes to my taste in music. In highschool, I was particular about choosing who and what I was listenting to because I felt I had to be unique and stand out (I never put myself out there regardless). I would search for niche tracks, particularly under the alternative umbrella. They were never that great sounding, but I liked feeling "on the cutting edge" [of nothing I'd ever enjoy now]...
I ended up boring myself pretty quickly. I still kept up the act because mr friends seemed to like the reccomendations I'd give them. I wanted everyone to believe that was the real me.

That's Not The Entire Truth Though

The truth is I loved pop music, musicals, video-game and movie OST's, orchestrated works, all that good stuff. It was not popular to be into those things as a kid/teen in the 2010's. These days most wouldn't bat an eye. Back then I felt I couldn't be myself around my nerd friends or my lacrosse team. I was very afraid people would think I was gay. So I rejected it all.

As it turns out...

That didn't really stop me from turning out to be gay, LOL. It's been a long journey, but I've learned (maybe I still am) to love myself. I could never imagine being as brave as i thought I had to be in the past just to ever mention this to anyone or say it outloud. Now it's just a regular occurrence.

So how does pop music tie into all of this???

It doesn't, I'm just trying fill up the page. But seriously, I think there was a cute little combo going on with my own self acceptance, and being lucky enough to have people around me that have supported me, grown with me, and shared similar interests, no matter how different we are. It's been a subtle growth in myself to be comfortable and sing and dance to songs I never would have before. I felt robbed of my experiences because I limited myself before, but I will not anymore.

Twice

I mentioned Twice in my first project. Back then I had only listened to a couple of their songs. NOW I am deep diving into their discograpghy. Cutesy pop concepts are usually a little too much for me. HOWEVER, I think the girls and the team they have behind them do a great job of exploring new genres and concepts, and the cutesy music doesn't feel cringey to listen to (for the most part). I'm obsessed with them now and it's all because I happened to take a chance and click on a link to this song. I have not regretted it in the slightest. I hope to see them on their next world tour and I'm excited for my first comeback as a new fan!

-Greg
