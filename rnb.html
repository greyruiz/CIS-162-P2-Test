Why I Love R&B...

Like most people born in the late 90's our mommas were probaly big fans of Hip Hop and R&B. Early weekday mornings were routine. My mom would take me to work at 5am in my pajamas (she was the director of the preschool I attended) and let me sleep. I just remember being teleported to the car. Like clockwork Alicia Keys would be playing on the radio, and that's how I knew it was time for school.

My mom was so fresh.

Her and my dad were both DJ's. Paid whether it was paid gigs or just for fun, it didn't really matter as long as they got to party. She's such a cool human being in my eyes. Outgoing, talkative, bright (the exact opposite of me LOL), she had a big influence on the music I enjoy today. While I love some old school or classic R&B, I find contemporary music to be the sweet spot for me. I Like songs with impact. I usually describe that pure moment that makes your arm hairs tingle as a metaphorical flower blooming. When everything comes together and the flower blooms I LIVE. It's such a cool feeling and I love to listen to new music and search out that particular sweet sound I love. It could be a single small moment, or one big one. I find that I most often get this insane euphoric feeling from R&B. The chords really do hit just right.

GallantxBrasstracks

I'm very fond of first time experiences. I'm a very sentimental person. Back in college, I loved watching people dance. Some of the performers I watched had insane musicality. Getting into the world of dance I suddenly had discovered a huge chunk of new music I never heard before. I was addicted to the sound and movement, so I started watching tournaments and special performances. The first time I had watched a VDC Video, I was blown away. I had discovered 2 of some of my favorite artists that day. Gallant's voice and Brasstracks silky instrumental gripped me in. I love them both so much. They always deliver on ear candy. They're my comfort artists.

-Greg
