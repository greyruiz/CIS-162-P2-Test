Here's Some of My Favorite Artists!

(Heads Up!.. Clicking Links Will Open Youtube In A New tab!)
Lake Street Dive
  • Jazz, Pop music, Indie pop, Blue-eyed soul, Southern rock

My first experience with LSD was a live cover of "I Want You Back" by The Jackson 5. I was 15 years old at the time, which makes me think how insanely fast time flies by (I'm 26 now). ANYWAY, I was blown away! This was not really a style and genre I was oppossed to, but I had never gone out of my way to listen to any contemporary artists in this category. I'd watched that video so many times back then (and apparently so did a lot of other people!! lol, they were definitely not pulling those numbers back in the day). I was so happy to hear they were releasing a full album (Bad Self Portraits), I ended up preordering the vinyl. After all these years and albums it still has a special place in my heart. It taught me to be open to a greater variety of sounds and got me through plenty good and bad days. They're an extremely talented group (did I mention the lead vocalist sings like an angel???) that deserves nothing but absolute success.

Listen To "Better Than"
Gallant
  • Alternative R&B

My personal favorite artist (top .05% listener on Spotify 👀)! Insane vocal range and a surprisingly diverse discography for mainly being an R&B singer. Ology [to me] is a once-in-a-lifetime album that I recommend most listen to at least once. Lyrical poetry that isn't pretentious + powerful vocals. The sound may not be for everyone, but that's okay because I enjoy it regardless :) I'll recommend a song off Neptune since that EP is a little less 'experimental'?? Not sure if that's the word I'm looking for... I've never taken music theory.

Listen To "Julie"
Vulfpeck
  • Funk

I remember having just flown in from school for Christmas back in 2014. I was scrolling on twitter, and a small creator I followed made a passing comment on how this band makes music that sounds like it belongs in 'Mario Kart'. I had to hear what he was talking about. While his claims didn't quite ring true, I ended up alling in love with this band one ep at a time. And only days later they released their first album, "The Thrill of The Arts". I have never stopped enjoying their music since the day I first played their songs. "Mr. Finish Line" has to be my favirite album from them. All vocalists get to shine and every track oozes passion. However, I have to recommend the OG, the song eveyone gets to vibe to no matter what their musical preferance is. TIMELESS!!!

Listen To "Wait for the Moment"
Jon Bellion
  • Pop, Hip-Hop, Contemporary R&B, Indie Rock

Kind of an outlier on this list. I was scrolling through twitter and saw a video of Jon at a pre-release fan meetup for his new album (The Human Condition). Pop music at the time really wasn't for me. He had already been wildy successful as a song writer for a lot of popular pop artists, but when it came to his original music he was relatively unknown outside of New York (bar harddcore fans). It took me a few listens to really get his angle. Fast forward and I've been to 2 concerts (it would have been 3 if i didn't mix up the date) and have enjoyed. I know he's been featured in a couple popular mainstream songs (Illenium's "Good Things Fall Apart" comes to mind), but I'm really just interested in his own personal discography. I can't say he's for everyone, but if you're a fan of pop there's a good chance you'll enjoy at least a couple of songs from his discography :)

Listen To "Blu (Acoustic)"

FKJ
  • Nu Jazz, Electronic, R&B, French House

Without realizing it I had consumed so much of this man's music in my late teen's up to now. Songs I could remember the melody of but not the name, or maybe a remix of a song from an artist I liked. Insanely talented and a unique sound like no other, I've really grown and had the pleasure to appreciate his art in the past couple of years. Time dissapears when i listen to his music, it's so comforting. I feel like I'd be doing a disservice to not include his live session of his EP "Ylang Ylang".
